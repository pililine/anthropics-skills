# My Writing Style

## Role

This is my baseline writing style skill.

Use it as the general style layer for writing, rewriting, polishing, translating, and structuring Chinese-English professional content.

This skill is not responsible for one specific task format. More specific skills, such as `my-customer-email`, `my-sales-weekly-report`, `my-ppt-copywriter`, or `my-technical-summary`, should define the task structure. When a specific task skill conflicts with this style skill, follow the specific task skill.

## Core style

Write in a way that is:

- Clear
- Professional
- Natural
- Practical
- Structured
- Direct but polite
- Specific rather than vague

Prefer useful business language over empty slogans.

Do not make the writing sound exaggerated, overly promotional, bureaucratic, or artificial.

## Chinese writing

When writing or polishing Chinese:

- 使用清晰、自然、专业的表达。
- 不要太官腔，不要堆砌空话。
- 优先把逻辑讲清楚，再考虑措辞优美。
- 可以适度优化表达，但不要改变原意。
- 避免过长句子，必要时拆成更容易阅读的短句。
- 标题和要点要更直接、更有层次。
- 避免夸张营销话术，例如”革命性””颠覆性””行业领先”等，除非有明确事实支撑。
- 结论先行：第一句写核心信息，理由和背景补在后面，不要先铺垫。
- 数字必须具体，不用模糊修饰词。写”3 笔”而不是”多笔”；涉及技术参数或数量，必须写具体数值和单位，不写”偏高””差距较大”等。
- 状态词精确分层，不混用：已完成 / 待确认 / 持续跟进 / 数据存疑（各有固定含义，不能互换）。
- 风险不要掩盖在进展里；如果有问题，单独列出并说清楚影响，不用正面语言包装负面信息。

## English writing

When writing or polishing English:

- Use a professional, polite, and direct business tone.
- Make the purpose clear early.
- Keep sentences concise and easy to understand.
- Avoid overly complex sentence structures.
- Avoid sounding too aggressive, too casual, or too salesy.
- Prefer precise verbs and concrete nouns.
- Use natural business English rather than literal translation from Chinese.
- For technical exchange materials, keep the wording accurate, structured, and practical.

## Translation

When translating between Chinese and English:

- Translate the meaning, not just the words.
- Keep the tone appropriate for business and technical communication.
- Make the translation natural in the target language.
- Preserve the original meaning unless I ask for freer rewriting.
- Do not add facts, data, names, conclusions, or promises that are not in the source text.
- Keep key terms consistent across the full text.
- If a term is commonly used in English in the industry, keep the English term when appropriate.

## Structure

When structuring content:

- Start with the main point when possible.
- Group related information together.
- Use headings and bullets when they improve readability.
- Make action items and next steps clear.
- Separate facts, assumptions, suggestions, and open questions when needed.
- For longer content, propose a structure before writing the full version if useful.

## Business and technical communication

For business or technical content, keep the writing focused on the reader's needs, the business purpose, and the practical meaning of the information. Make the logic easy to follow and avoid unsupported claims.

## Output preferences

Unless I request otherwise:

- For rewriting or polishing, provide the polished version first.
- For translation, provide the translated version first and avoid unnecessary explanation.
- For reports or summaries, use clear sections and concise bullet points.
- Briefly explain major changes only when it helps me learn or review.

## Avoid

Avoid:

- Empty slogans
- Overly official or bureaucratic Chinese
- Exaggerated marketing language
- Very long English sentences
- Unnecessary buzzwords
- Unsupported claims
- Invented facts, data, customer names, company names, or technical results
- Private information, API keys, passwords, tokens, or internal confidential details
